A notary public is a public servant appointed by a state authorities. The general focus of his or her task is to witness the finalizing of documents and administer oaths. They serve to hinder fraud, appearing as a neutral witness for legal files such as affidavits, deeds or powers of attorney. The existence of a notary public assists to screen for imposters and make certain both parties are participating in a contract purposefully and voluntarily.

Historically, notary publics go back numerous centuries to the Roman Empire. Originally, they where selected by the Pope, who would entrust his powers of appointment to the Archbishop of Canterbury.

Certain legal documents need the existence of a notary when they are signed. Other times private business or individuals may employ the services of a notary in order to enhance the validity and safeguard taking part celebrations from fraud.

Some states require that the notary public exist at the time of a document's finalizing to be acknowledged as legitimate, however it is more frequently the case that the private should merely appear before the notary and provide evidence of identity to have a file notarized.

For proof of identification, a notary typically needs a file with photo, description and signature. Common examples would be a driver's license, passport or military ID.

Regardless of the fact that notary publics typically handle delicate legal documents, they are not accredited to give legal representation, counsel or services in any way. Therefore they can have no part in the preparation of any files to be notarized.

For those looking for to become a notary public, obtaining a license is a relatively basic job. People need little unique training or prior knowledge, and are only needed to pass a test and undergo some form of background check, although, particular laws governing the appointment of notaries can vary from state to state.
